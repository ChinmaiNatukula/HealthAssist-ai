🛡️ Health Precautions

Preventive health guidelines, diet suggestions, and lifestyle tips for common conditions.

🥗 Nutrition

🍎 Healthy Diet Tips

A balanced diet is the foundation of good health and disease prevention.

  • Fill half your plate with colorful vegetables and fruits
  • Choose whole grains over refined/processed grains
  • Include lean protein: eggs, fish, legumes, tofu
  • Drink 8–10 glasses of water daily
  • Limit sugar, salt, and saturated/trans fats
  • Eat 3 balanced meals; avoid skipping breakfast
  • Limit ultra-processed foods and sugary drinks
🏃 Activity

💪 Exercise & Lifestyle

Regular physical activity prevents chronic diseases and boosts mental health.

  • Aim for 150 min/week of moderate aerobic exercise
  • Include strength training at least 2x per week
  • Take short walks after meals to regulate blood sugar
  • Get 7–9 hours of quality sleep nightly
  • Reduce sedentary time — stand/move every 30 min
  • Quit smoking; limit alcohol to moderate amounts
  • Manage stress with meditation, yoga, or deep breathing
💊 Prevention

❤️ Heart Disease Prevention

Cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of death — but highly preventable.

  • Monitor and control blood pressure (target: below 120/80)
  • Keep LDL cholesterol in healthy range through diet
  • Exercise aerobically for 30+ min most days
  • Maintain healthy body weight (BMI 18.5–24.9)
  • Avoid smoking — it doubles heart attack risk
  • Limit sodium intake to under 2,300 mg/day
  • Get annual cardiovascular check-ups after age 40
🧬 Chronic

🩸 Diabetes Management

Diabetes is manageable with consistent lifestyle changes and monitoring.

  • Monitor blood glucose levels before and after meals
  • Follow a low-glycemic index (GI) diet
  • Exercise regularly — it improves insulin sensitivity
  • Take medications/insulin consistently as prescribed
  • Get HbA1c test every 3 months
  • Inspect feet daily for cuts/sores (prevents complications)
  • Avoid sugary drinks, white rice, and processed foods
🧠 Mental

🧘 Mental Health & Wellbeing

Mental health is just as important as physical health. Prioritize it.

  • Practice mindfulness or meditation for 10 min daily
  • Maintain social connections with friends and family
  • Talk to a therapist or counselor when overwhelmed
  • Set healthy work-life boundaries and take breaks
  • Limit social media consumption to reduce anxiety
  • Regular exercise significantly reduces depression symptoms
  • Seek help — mental illness is treatable, not a weakness
🦠 Infection

😷 Infection Prevention

Simple hygiene practices dramatically reduce the spread of infectious diseases.

  • Wash hands for 20+ seconds with soap before meals
  • Stay up to date with recommended vaccinations
  • Cover mouth/nose when coughing or sneezing
  • Avoid touching eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands
  • Disinfect frequently touched surfaces regularly
  • Wear a mask when sick in public spaces
  • Cook food to proper temperatures; avoid raw/undercooked meat
☀️ Skin

🧴 Skin Health & Sun Safety

Proper skin care prevents infections, early aging, and skin cancers.

  • Apply broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen every day
  • Moisturize daily, especially after bathing
  • Avoid prolonged sun exposure between 10 AM – 4 PM
  • Perform monthly self-skin checks for new or changing moles
  • Stay hydrated — dehydration affects skin elasticity
  • Use fragrance-free, hypoallergenic products for sensitive skin
  • See a dermatologist annually for a professional skin cancer screening
🚨 Emergency

🚑 When to Seek Emergency Care

Recognizing emergencies early can save lives. Don't delay — call for help.

  • Chest pain or pressure — possible heart attack (102/108)
  • Sudden severe headache with stiff neck — possible meningitis
  • Difficulty breathing or choking
  • Sudden loss of vision, speech, or movement — possible stroke
  • Uncontrolled bleeding or deep wounds
  • Overdose or poisoning — call Poison Control immediately
  • Loss of consciousness or severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
👁️ Screening

🏥 Preventive Screenings

Regular health check-ups detect diseases early when they're most treatable.

  • Annual blood pressure check for all adults over 18
  • Fasting blood glucose every 3 years from age 35
  • Lipid panel (cholesterol) every 5 years from age 20
  • Eye exam every 1–2 years, especially for diabetics
  • Dental check-up every 6 months
  • Women: cervical pap smear every 3 years from age 21
  • Colon cancer screening from age 45–50 as recommended
